Who I Am

I'm a final-year B.Tech student specializing in AI/ML, computer networks, and software engineering. I build things that are technically rigorous and actually useful — not just demo toys.

My final-year capstone focuses on Parkinson's Disease detection using a multi-modal deep learning architecture — combining voice, handwriting, and imaging data, with explainability tools like Grad-CAM and SHAP to make predictions interpretable in clinical contexts.

Outside of academics, I'm deep into algorithm design, systems programming, and exploring the lower layers of computing — from BSP trees to custom encryption schemes. I believe in understanding fundamentals before reaching for frameworks.
